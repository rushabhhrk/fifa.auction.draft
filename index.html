📋 Tournament Overview
1
Each captain receives ₹1,50,00,000 (₹1.5 Crore) as their auction purse. This single budget covers your partner, all lot bids, and all hammer purchases. Once it's gone, it's gone.
2
Phase 0 — Partner Auction: Before the draft, each captain bids for one partner from the non-captain participant pool. Your partner co-manages your squad every match.
3
Phase 1 — Lot Bidding: 8 lots (by position) are auctioned. All 6 captains bid on every lot. Highest bidder picks first from that lot; lowest bidder picks last and pays the average of all 6 bids. Each captain gets exactly 1 player per lot.
4
Phase 2 — Hammer Round: Sub-87-rated players (+ GK overflow) auctioned one by one, position by position. Highest bid wins. Open live bidding — no sealed bids.
5
Final Squad: 16–18 players total (including partner). Minimum requirements: 1 GK, 4 DEF, 4 MID, 3 ATT. Fill the rest as you choose.
🪙 The Consolation Token

The captain who bids lowest in any lot pays the average of all 6 bids (not their own lower bid) — but receives a Consolation Token. This token can be used exactly once during the Hammer Round to match the leading bid on any player and win them outright. Each captain can hold maximum one token at any time. Tokens cannot be traded or saved across sessions.

How the Hammer Works

Auctioneer calls each player: GK → Defenders → Midfielders → Wingers/CAM → Strikers. Captains shout bids live until the gavel falls. Consolation Token can be played here — holder can match any bid and take the player immediately. Minimum bid = base price listed.

🤝 Partner Auction Rules
1
The Partner Pool consists of all participants who are not captains. Names are announced before bidding starts.
2
Partners are called one by one. Any captain may open bidding. Highest bid wins. Open live bidding — no sealed bids.
3
Minimum bid: ₹5,00,000 (₹5 Lakh). No cap. Bid whatever you can afford.
4
Partner spend is deducted from your ₹1.5 Crore purse immediately. Track it on the Purse tab.
5
Your partner co-manages your squad on a second controller in every 2v2 match. Choose someone whose playstyle complements yours.
6
If a partner receives no bids, they are assigned to the captain with the lowest remaining purse at no cost.
💡 Partner Hint

Your partner shapes how you play every single match. Think about defensive awareness, communication under pressure, and whether they track back. Someone with great game sense is often worth more than the highest-rated player in the lots.

Strategy Corner
Hints to give you an edge — not a recipe, just the right questions
🎯
System First, Players Second
Before a single bid is placed, decide what formation you want to run. Don't let the auction shape your style — let your style shape the auction. Captains who drift end up with squads that don't know what they are.
🧤
The Goalkeeper Is Always Undervalued
Every captain is eyeing the Attacker lots. The GK lot quietly sets the ceiling on your team's defensive ability. A top-tier keeper wins you points no striker ever could. Most people realise this too late.
🏗️
Depth Beats Stars
One 91-rated player doesn't win you a tournament. Eight solid 84s might. A deep squad handles rotations, rematches, and positional flexibility. Don't empty the purse chasing a single name.
🪙
The Token Is a Late-Game Weapon
If you hold the Consolation Token, don't show your hand. The perfect moment is a hammer player who fills your squad's weakest position — when every other captain wants them just as much.
👥
Partner Fit Over Partner Fame
A well-known gamer as a partner looks impressive on paper. But someone who stays disciplined, tracks back, and communicates under pressure wins you matches. Assess their game IQ, not just their name.
🔍
Watch What the Room Wants
If three other captains are desperately chasing the same position, let them drain their purses. Step back, find value elsewhere, and return when the dust settles. There are no bad players — only wrong prices.
📉
Late Lots Are Where Value Hides
Auction fatigue is real. By Lot 6 or 7, purses are tighter and attention slips. The captain who conserves early and attacks late often walks away with the best value in the entire draft.
⚡
Don't Arrive at Hammer Empty
The hammer round isn't a consolation prize — it's where most squads are actually completed. Many 84–86 rated players punch above their weight in 2v2. Reserve budget. Arrive dangerous.
🎭
The Bid Bluff
Overbidding on a lot you don't actually need forces rivals to overpay. Used once or twice it can break a rival's budget before the lot you really want. Used too often, it breaks yours. It's a scalpel.
🧮
Know Your Floor Before You Enter
Calculate your minimum spend before the auction: 8 lots at base + partner minimum + ~8 hammer picks. Know the floor, and you know exactly how much you can gamble above it.
🌟
The Mix Lot Is a Wild Card
The Mix Lot has the most positionally versatile players — most can play two or three roles. If you have a gap heading into the final lots, the mix lot might be the cleanest way to patch it.
🏆
Chemistry Compounds Over Time
Same-club players connect more naturally in 2v2. Where possible, thread in a club chain or positional link through your squad — it compounds into better chemistry and smoother co-op movement.
