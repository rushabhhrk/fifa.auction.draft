🗓️ Match Format & Weekly Schedule
1
League Phase — Double Round-Robin: Each team plays every other team twice (home and away). 5 matchdays total, 2–3 matches per matchday.
2
Knockout Phase: Top 4 teams advance. Two-legged Semi-finals (aggregate score decides who goes through) → Grand Final (single match, winner takes all).
3
Weekly Schedule: One fixed day per week agreed by the group (e.g., Friday). Both matches of the day happen in the same session.
4
Match Duration: 5-minute halves (10 min total). Can be adjusted to 7-min halves by group vote before the season.
5
Points: Win = 3 pts · Draw = 1 pt · Loss = 0 pts.
Tiebreaker order: Goal Difference → Goals Scored → Head-to-Head. No coin flips — if still level, the higher-rated GK's team gets precedence.
📅 Reschedule & Absence Rules
1
One reschedule per team per season. Either captain may invoke it. The new day is proposed and confirmed by group vote.
2
Post-reschedule absence: If a team still doesn't show after their reschedule is used, they receive a ₹200 fine (deducted from winnings / paid upfront) and the match is recorded as a 0–3 forfeit.

🔄 Transfer Window — Week 3
📅

Opens after Week 3 matchday · Closes before Week 4

Your one chance to reshape the squad mid-season. All transfers must be announced in the group chat and go unchallenged for 1 hour before becoming official.

🤝
Partner Trade: Two captains may swap partners if both agree. Each captain can exercise this option once per season only. No forced trades.
🔀
Player Transfers: Up to 3 players per team can be exchanged. Both captains involved must agree on the swap. No cash changes hands — straight player-for-player.
🛒
Unsold Player Pickups: Players not bought at auction are available from the pool. Price = that player's hammer base price. Max 2 unsold pickups per team, deducted from remaining purse.
🔒
Window closed = squad locked. No transfers once knockouts begin. Whatever you built is what you fight with.

📋 Tournament Overview
1
Each captain receives ₹1,50,00,000 (₹1.5 Crore) as their auction purse. This budget covers your partner and all player purchases. Once it's gone, it's gone.
2
Phase 0 — Partner Auction: Before the draft, each captain bids for one partner from the non-captain participant pool. Your partner co-manages your squad every match.
3
Phase 1 — Hammer Round: All players auctioned one by one, position by position (GK → DEF → MID → ATT). Auctioneer calls each player — captains shout bids live until the gavel falls. Highest bid wins. Base price ₹5L for every player. Minimum increment ₹50,000.
4
Final Squad: 16–18 players total (including partner). Minimum requirements: 1 GK, 4 DEF, 4 MID, 3 ATT. Fill the rest as you choose.

🤝 Partner Auction Rules
1
The Partner Pool consists of all participants who are not captains. Names are announced before bidding starts.
2
Partners are called one by one. Any captain may open bidding. Highest bid wins. Open live bidding — no sealed bids.
3
Minimum bid: ₹5,00,000 (₹5 Lakh). No cap. Bid whatever you can afford.
4
Partner spend is deducted from your ₹1.5 Crore purse immediately. Track it on the Purse tab.
5
Your partner co-manages your squad on a second controller in every 2v2 match. Choose someone whose playstyle complements yours.
6
If a partner receives no bids, they are assigned to the captain with the lowest remaining purse at no cost.
💡 Partner Hint

Your partner shapes how you play every single match. Think about defensive awareness, communication under pressure, and whether they track back. Someone with great game sense is often worth more than the highest-rated player in the lots.

🗺️ Overall Tournament Strategy
📅
Week 1–2 — Establish early points. Your squad is freshest and opponents haven't studied your style yet. Don't play conservative football here — attack with full intensity and bank 6 points before the league gets competitive.
🔄
Week 3 — Transfer Window is a weapon. Use the first two matchdays to identify your squad's weakest position. The transfer window opens after Week 3. If you know what you need going in, you can target the exact unsold player or propose the right trade before rivals do.
📊
Track the Golden Boot race. If your striker is in contention, protect their minutes — substitute late, don't rest them needlessly. A Golden Boot winner adds prestige and cements your legacy in the tournament.
🤝
Partner communication is the differentiator. In 2v2, one player is always out of position. The teams that win are the ones calling switches, defending together, and not blaming each other when it goes wrong. Chemistry off the pitch = performance on it.
🏆
Knockout football is a different game. What wins you 3 points in the league might not win a one-off knockout tie. Have a plan for managing the match — when to hold a lead, when to go for the second goal, and how to handle penalties if it comes to that.
🎭
Study your opponents' squads before you play them. Every captain's squad is visible after the auction. You know their best player, their weak flank, and whether they have pace up front. Prepare a tactical counter before the match — not during it.
💸
The reschedule is valuable — don't waste it early. Hold your reschedule for a matchday where you genuinely can't both be available. A forfeit in a close league table can be the difference between 3rd and 5th place.
🎯 Auction-Day Tips
🎯
System First, Players Second
Before a single bid is placed, decide what formation you want to run. Don't let the auction shape your style — let your style shape the auction. Captains who drift end up with squads that don't know what they are.
🧤
The Goalkeeper Is Always Undervalued
Every captain is eyeing the Attacker lots. The GK lot quietly sets the ceiling on your team's defensive ability. A top-tier keeper wins you points no striker ever could. Most people realise this too late.
🏗️
Depth Beats Stars
One 91-rated player doesn't win you a tournament. Eight solid 84s might. A deep squad handles rotations, rematches, and positional flexibility. Don't empty the purse chasing a single name.
👥
Partner Fit Over Partner Fame
A well-known gamer as a partner looks impressive on paper. But someone who stays disciplined, tracks back, and communicates under pressure wins you matches. Assess their game IQ, not just their name.
🔍
Watch What the Room Wants
If three other captains are desperately chasing the same position, let them drain their purses. Step back, find value elsewhere, and return when the dust settles. There are no bad players — only wrong prices.
📉
Late Lots Are Where Value Hides
Auction fatigue is real. By Lot 6 or 7, purses are tighter and attention slips. The captain who conserves early and attacks late often walks away with the best value in the entire draft.
⚡
Don't Arrive at Hammer Empty
The hammer round isn't a consolation prize — it's where most squads are actually completed. Many 84–86 rated players punch above their weight in 2v2. Reserve budget. Arrive dangerous.
🎭
The Bid Bluff
Overbidding on a lot you don't actually need forces rivals to overpay. Used once or twice it can break a rival's budget before the lot you really want. Used too often, it breaks yours. It's a scalpel.
🧮
Know Your Floor Before You Enter
Calculate your minimum spend before the auction: 8 lots at base + partner minimum + ~8 hammer picks. Know the floor, and you know exactly how much you can gamble above it.
🌟
The Mix Lot Is a Wild Card
The Mix Lot has the most positionally versatile players — most can play two or three roles. If you have a gap heading into the final lots, the mix lot might be the cleanest way to patch it.
🏆
Chemistry Compounds Over Time
Same-club players connect more naturally in 2v2. Where possible, thread in a club chain or positional link through your squad — it compounds into better chemistry and smoother co-op movement.
